Finding the Optimal Number of Clusters using the Elbow Method

In[58]:
from sklearn.cluster import KMeans
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Selecting only the numeric columns well use for clustering
X = df[['Annual Income (k$)', 'Spending Score (1-100)']]

# List to store WCSS values for different k
wcss = []

# Trying k values from 1 to 10
for i in range(1, 11):
    kmeans = KMeans(n_clusters=i, init='k-means++', random_state=42)
    kmeans.fit(X)
    wcss.append(kmeans.inertia_)  # inertia_ gives WCSS

# Plotting the results
plt.figure(figsize=(8,5))
plt.plot(range(1, 11), wcss, marker='o', linestyle='--')
plt.title('The Elbow Method')
plt.xlabel('Number of clusters (k)')
plt.ylabel('WCSS')
plt.show()

Apply K-Means Clustering

In[61]:
from sklearn.cluster import KMeans
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Applying K-Means with 5 clusters
kmeans = KMeans(n_clusters=5, init='k-means++', random_state=42)
y_kmeans = kmeans.fit_predict(X)

# Add the cluster column to the original dataset
df['Cluster'] = y_kmeans

Conclusion

Conclusion In this project, we successfully applied Unsupervised Machine Learning using the K-Means Clustering algorithm on the Mall Customers Dataset to identify distinct customer segments. Through the Elbow Method, we determined that the optimal number of clusters was five, representing five different types of customers based on their Annual Income and Spending Score. After analyzing and visualizing the results, the clusters can be interpreted as follows: 1. Cluster 0 Low Income, Low Spenders: Customers in this group have low income and spend less. They can be targeted through basic offers or seasonal discounts. 2. Cluster 1 Average Income, Average Spenders: These are middle-class, stable customers who respond well to regular promotions and loyalty programs. 3. Cluster 2 High Income, High Spenders: Premium customers with strong purchasing power. Personalized offers, premium memberships, and exclusive events would engage them effectively. 4. Cluster 3 High Income, Low Spenders: Affluent but cautious spenders. Building trust, emphasizing quality, and brand reputation can convert them into loyal customers. 5. Cluster 4 Low Income, High Spenders: Budget-conscious yet brand-loving customers who spend more than their income level suggests. They are ideal for marketing trendy but affordable products. Through this project, we learned how clustering algorithms can help businesses understand customer behavior, personalize marketing strategies, and make data-driven decisions. This unsupervised learning model showcases how data analytics can turn raw data into actionable business insights, supporting smarter and more targeted decision-making
